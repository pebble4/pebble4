Water Pollution

Previous Next

1-Many of the postcard-perfect blue waters that make Florida a tourist mecca are coming up green and choked with nasty, toxic algae.

2-It has happened that horrified tourists and residents watch as the waves toss up hundreds of dead fish.

3-The culprit behind this environmental and economic crisis is pollution caused by inadequately treated sewage, manure and fertilizer.

4-The way to make Florida’s waters clean again is to limit this pollution at its source.

5-Excess nitrogen and phosphorus spur these toxic algae outbreaks.

6-The fact is, industrial polluters have been using public waters as their cheap, private sewers for years.

7-This pollution is poisoning the rivers, lakes and streams that supply the water to kitchen taps.

8-Floridians deserve clean drinking water, not water polluted with sewage, fertilizer and manure runoff.

9-It is time for Florida to establish limits on the water pollution that threatens families’ health and drinking water.

Now that we know more about how the nitrogen and phosphorus in sewage, manure and fertilizer tip Florida’s delicate ecological balance, we have a responsibility to do something about it.
