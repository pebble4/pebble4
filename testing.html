Standardized Testing

Throughout a student's school career they're tested from grade to grade, subject to subject created by the state. The majority of these tests determine if a student is promoted to the next grade or not. It also plays an important part of the school's grade and sometimes even the teacher's wages.

SOO...

So you see there's a chain of people that Standardized Testing affects but who does most of the pressure fall on??? The students. The students already have to worry about repeating a grade if they don't pass and then they pressure put on them by teachers whose wages and evaluation will be affect by their students performance along with pressure from school administration who want to raise their school grade or maintain their grade.

    Why do we find it to be so bad

  • Students who fail these test have to take intensive classes in higher grades.
  • Pacing guides are sometimes unrealistic and teachers fall behind and have to rush and cram weeks and days before testdates or teacher follow the pacing guide but the pacing guides are to fast and students don't really learn anything and aren't prepared for the test
  • What teachers are told to teach the students isn't what's on the test
