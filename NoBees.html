No Bees, No Food


Previous Next

1-Millions of bees are dying off, with alarming consequences for our environment and our food supply.

2-We rely on bees to pollinate everything from almonds to strawberries to the hay used to feed dairy cows.

3- What happens if the bees disappear? It’s simple: No bees, no food.

4-In recent years, beekeepers report they’re losing on average 30% of all honeybee colonies each winter — twice the loss considered economically tolerable.

5-We rely on bees to pollinate 71 of the 100 crops that provide 90% of most of the world’s food.

6-Scientists point to several causes behind the problem, including global warming, habitat loss,

parasites and a class of bee-killing insecticides known as neonicotinoids (or neonics).

7-Right now, we’re letting big agrichemical companies use more of the chemicals that are known to kill bees just as we’re in the midst of an unsustainable die-off in bee populations.

8-Big agrichemical companies are fighting to prevent bans and has asked federal regulators for permission to use even larger quantities of these pesticides.

That has to change now!

Together, we can give bees a chance.
